Talking point: Test¶

Testing the intermediate steps as resolved / included in the project is important !

Fig. 25 Test as steps proceed, but make some paper trails & code samples too¶

Source code of tiny fragments of code that work help co-workers get going on new functionality. The time spent to get this right helps avoid the bog down-ness problem as the final goals are close. A well managed product development process, gets slowly easier as the end approaches.

This is hard to achieve, but not impossible.

Talking point: Topaz Tower¶

Sometimes people not around the earlier moments of techno product development, don't have much of an idea where things ``come from''.

A bad idea is a prototyping sequence of prototypes that look right, and are hard to use for development.

A rough looking thing completely; Yet 100% functional, exposing an ideal product to the INTERNET side on a VPN.

This is a typical exceedingly important step.

Looking like the real thing is great, but that without functionality is a trap.

Fig. 26 A very serious, disposable Fixture¶

It's like the INTERNET Meme, ``On the net, nobody knows your a dog''.

This one for instance ``went'' to trade shows along with perfect looking boxes that were non-functional. The customers were told that, no secrecy, and nobody cares, at all.

Not being afraid of this step, is important. A fancy looking, ``One-off'' that everybody is afraid to alter, is a crazy bad idea that looks nice.
