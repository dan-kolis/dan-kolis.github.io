Heads up - Dan K and building technology¶

Heading about Two_setpointers¶

In 2017, A few interests of mine collided with a professional request for a friend and I found myself making a one of breadboard for a HVAC fan controller. That's the little handmade thing on the left. in this photo.

Results with that were very satisfactory. The analog setting of a simple pot compared to a programmable logic controller is a remarkable shortcut to this functionality. Its also cheaper, but the energy savings eclipses the delta of the initial costs anway.

Two_setpointers caption here¶

The company that deploys these sees electronics as a inconvenience; both parties did as minimal of a job as human beings can do on every line item.

There's no documentation cycle, no feedback, no site information. The only transaction is how much and when, followed by a count as the quantity of 100 or so was depleted.

Heading about Three_power¶

The idea of having initial quantities deployed in real building really excited me. The endless arguing over what they costs and so on was the usual noise of building automation people.

But early on I was, and still am, much more interested in the big picture of building automation, and that is way beyond some voltage setting set-points for fans.

Three_power caption here¶

The notion the Hydronic fan stuff is a ramp to an integration and the powering ideas should be really tried has led nowwhere.

Even the fan coil support part is a failure; Every transaction is a new kid of horror.

Heading about Tab_connectors¶

People used to computers and servers have a tough time focusing on what matters greatly to people doing building wiring.

It's simply incredible how large quantities are for any design win. One building, the Burj Kalifa in Qatar has 80K PLC's for HVAC.

Tab_connectors caption here¶

So with maybe fifteen connections at two ends, this is more then two million endpoints. So every issue like reliability and cost, reduction in wiring errors, and efficiency matter greatly.

Because this particular deliverable set was only hundreds, it doesn't twitch the meter to these insiders as a real project.

Heading about Power_doer¶

This PCB was my second using a program called kiCad for making PCBs. I've gotten reasonably capable at it generally.

Any PCB is a sad story on the first pass. If the first PCB can be used at all, its a success. The second and Nth is much, much easier.

Power_doer caption here¶

This one integrates powering for analog setting for three driven elements and this one was made with a fan, and probably a light as a driven element. This would typically be a LED fixture.

The idea this isn't just a sub-contract to run some dumb fans is more apparent incrementally, of course.

Heading about Heaped_pcb¶

In 2019 the initial quantities were depleted so another pass was manufactured. Fancy people get excited over Surface mount versus through hole boards, end users to dot care in the slightest.

What they cost, if there immune to being burned out and what kind of connectors and wires are required interests them much more.

Heading about Nicole_sidewalker¶

The notion is to combine HVAC, Low-voltage wiring, and lighting in an integrated system. Has enormous advantages for building management. Nicole and I talked about it a few times; She was a V.P. of Alphabet, Googles parent company posted here at Toronto Sidewalk.

She has a background in V.C. and was deeply involved in planning for Sidewalk.

Nicole_sidewalker caption here¶

Her intense inquiries into the ideas with no likelyhood of getting a real project where annoying, but of course, she knew at the time Sidewalk was in huge trouble with its masters. Also, with years at Sand Hill in San Fran, she was used to squeezing hard to fine out the notions under things.

Her co-worker invented all the products for a company called Lutron for low voltage wiring.

He was beat up horribly by management, but now the products are world leaders in this.

Nicole now for for Creative Destruction at U of T.

Heading about Bright_hall¶

I studied this hall in First Canadian Place extensively; I counted every person for hours and made a computer model of savings to cut light sometimes up to 20 percent.

I got to first base with a manager in the building, but the electrical contractor he assigned me completely rejected any low voltage work. Building sub-contractors generally hate L.V. work because they don't benefit from its operational aspects but often have failed projects using it even in the slightest.

Bright_hall caption here¶

Of course, a little PCB would be up in the ceiling running these lights in a completely distributed control system. This is allowable up to 28 Volts with no fussy rules whatsoever.

The idea HVAC and lighting could share a slim wiring system without fuseboxes and National Electrical Contractor (NEC) rules is quite frequently discussed by buildign people, but not actually tried much.

Heading about Just_light¶

This light has a sensor dangling off it and uses the wiring power and control slim technology.

Of course other ones where dressed up a little more, but insiders liek us like to see the way it works.

Just_light caption here¶

This single seonsor with a IOT micro can easily detect occupancy, and also do what is called ``daylighting'', to turn down light when sunlight is apparent.

To make it clear this worked, I also make the light dimmable with a gesture. There are some videos of that in private parts of You tube.

Important: There is a surprising amount of L.V. stuff that doesn't even require a CPU in the PCb's !

Heading about Light_floor¶

Studying the light as a typical control point for L.V. was interesting.

One unfortunate aspect of this was the recognition of the hand waving and dimming, etc. seemed kind of novel to people.

I would start by saying: ``The important point is the unseen unification of wiring and control'', and the stakeholder what say: ``Of course'', but I endlessly felt the playing with it was a distraction form the central point.

Light_floor caption here¶

Lighting and HVAC both overlap the same people space, especially hosting people detection is obviously useful.

California has all sorts of laws that new buildings require this stuff, but so little of it is actually deployed it's kind of bizarre.

Lots of L.V. systems are installed and turned off compeltely. There just there to get out of trouble with the authorities.

Heading about Gesture_micro¶

So here we go with the fun integration of the power driver and an IOT chip.

Gesture_micro caption here¶

I really worked hard to make the controller the absolute minimal part that could do the job.

This was controllable by RS-485 which is a robust, known good protocol. The Building Automation System (BAS) is a known well regarded standard, and it is usually consdered best to run sort of off line from internet CAT-5 completely.

The equipment manufacturers for Building automation have done such a remarkably bad job they manage to use gigbit highways and have it turn ligths on with weird delays.

They know nothing about computer science, I doubt most managers making this stuff know that not just comptuer programming of some sort.

Heading about Simple_mess¶

This is another order built of around a 120 contol points. Nothign but arguing over the costs...

Simple_mess caption here¶

Perhaps half of the building installs of these have been successful and the other half total failures.

One site found every savings wildly beyond there expectations, they could hardly believe cheap stuff could cut a whole building energy budget by 20 percent with ease.

Other companies in nearly the same situation technically had no ability to make anything work, so they just fired guys and ... who knows. Building people consider any sales that are fully paid and don't' turn into lawsuits are successes.

The alleged measurement of performance are utterly gamed to make building people get credentials and tax breaks; they are mostly gimmicks.

Heading about Streetlight_controller¶

Here is a dimming circuit for streetlights.

It's really important to grasp quantities, not mark-up is the money mover in these deployments.

Streetlight_controller caption here¶

Very simple circuits can sell by the hundreds of thousands or millions.

Fairly crummy performance is not too much of a problem, but reliability is essential for many pieces. This photo is here to just remind the reader, there are lots of deployments of low parts count simple power pieces without a micro in them.

Heading about Delta_controller¶

This is a commercial controller for (BACnet), the building control standard.

These look like PLC's but aren't. They often run on 24 VAC and have considerable fixed software fro HVAC.

Seemingly non have really figured out people use lights and HVAC in the same rooms.

Delta_controller caption here¶

There expensive usually for fancy box with one micro in them, this one's $500 or so.

Notice when there used, its without exception in every room etc in a entire building.

Lots of the companies that do this and live in the shadows under Honeywell and Siemens are in ``funnier countries'' like Canada and Scandinavia.

If they did better they would have been long ago been bought up by the majors, right ?

Delta is in Vancouver.

These miss a little I think by having no notion of the wiring system behind them at all. BACnet is medium crummy as a standard, but usable.

Heading about Honeywell_stat¶

HVAC seems to somehow turn into a nearly natural monopoly or sorts. Everybody knows this product line from Honeywell.

Honeywell_stat caption here¶

Will lots of fancy one's that do all sorts of remote control stuff its important to realize what even the fanciest one's don't do well:

Heading about Bagged_pcb¶

The PCBs are still vaccum packed, (easier to solder if not oxidized at all).

I think they will really hit the wall by December of 2021. And need these.

Bagged_pcb caption here¶

I did these on speculation entirely.

Building construction people enjoy engineered crisis, there is no way to discuss them without somebody threatening to sue somebody.

I made breadboards to test the ideas in these; There pretty likely to work well.

Each does 12 channels, not one ot three channels. This was done mostly to get the per channel cost down, but might have advantages.

I made a nice data sheet for it too. Nobody even looked at it.

Until there is a crisis, it doesn't count as existing.
